About KUJA

Khulna University Journalists’ Association (KUJA) was officially established in 2020 with the aim of creating a unified and professional platform for student journalists at Khulna University. Its formation marked a significant transition from individual journalistic activities to a coordinated and organized body representing campus-based media contributors.

From the beginning, KUJA has worked to promote responsible journalism, ensure the dissemination of accurate information, and highlight important academic, administrative, and student-related issues through various media outlets. The organization has also emphasized ethical reporting and constructive engagement between students and the university administration.

A defining feature of KUJA’s journey has been its democratic and evolving leadership. Over the years, the association has been guided by several presidents, each contributing to its institutional growth and visibility. The leadership progressed through figures such as Tehosin Ashraf, followed by Aniruddha Bishwas and Rezowan Ahmmed, and later by Ekramul Haque, continuing to Alkama Romin. Through these successive terms, KUJA has strengthened its organizational structure and expanded its activities.

Alongside its reporting role, KUJA has organized discussions, training sessions, and observance of journalism-related events, helping members develop professional skills and awareness. These initiatives have gradually established KUJA as a credible and active organization within the university.

As of 2026, KUJA has celebrated its 6th anniversary, marking a notable milestone in its relatively short but impactful history. Within this period, the association has grown into a strong platform for aspiring journalists, contributing meaningfully to campus discourse and the broader media landscape of Bangladesh.
